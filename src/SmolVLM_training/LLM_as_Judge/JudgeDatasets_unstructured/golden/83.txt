The graph illustrates the decline in the size of the U.S. active-duty military force from 1990 to 2015.

In 1990, the U.S. active-duty force comprised approximately 2.1 million personnel. This number saw a significant decline over the next decade, dropping to around 1.4 million by 2000. The downward trend continued, albeit at a slower pace, reaching its lowest point of approximately 1.3 million in 2015.

The reduction in military personnel was most pronounced during the 1990s, coinciding with the end of the Cold War and subsequent military downsizing. Although the decline slowed in the 2000s, the overall trend remained negative, reflecting ongoing adjustments in military strategy and force structure.

Overall, the data indicates a consistent decrease in the size of the U.S. active-duty military force over the 25-year period, highlighting significant shifts in defense policy and military requirements.

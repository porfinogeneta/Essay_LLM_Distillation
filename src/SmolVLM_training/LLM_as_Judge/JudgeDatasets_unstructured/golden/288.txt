Kiribati : Infant mortality rate from 2009 to 2019 ( in deaths per 1,000 live births )

The bar chart illustrates the infant mortality rate in Kiribati from 2009 to 2019, measured in deaths per 1,000 live births.

The data reveals a consistent decline in infant mortality rates over the decade. Starting at 49.9 deaths per 1,000 live births in 2009, the rate gradually decreased to 40.1 by 2019. This downward trend signifies an improvement in healthcare and living conditions over the years.

Initially, the rate dropped from 49.9 in 2009 to 48.5 in 2011, followed by a more pronounced decrease to 46.7 in 2013. The most significant reduction occurred between 2013 and 2015, where the rate fell from 46.7 to 44.6. Although the decline continued, the rate of decrease slowed down in the latter years, with the rate dropping from 43.5 in 2016 to 40.1 in 2019.

Overall, Kiribati has made substantial progress in reducing infant mortality over the past decade. The consistent decline in the rate of deaths per 1,000 live births reflects ongoing efforts to enhance healthcare services and improve overall living conditions. Despite the slower rate of decrease in recent years, the trend remains positive, indicating a continued commitment to improving infant health outcomes.

U.S. Hispanic Population in 2012
The graph illustrates the growth of the Hispanic population in the United States from 1970 to 2012.

In 1970, the Hispanic population in the U.S. was 9.1 million. This number increased steadily over the decades, reaching 14.6 million in 1980 and 22.4 million in 1990. By the year 2000, the population had grown to 35.3 million.

The upward trend continued into the new millennium, with the Hispanic population reaching 50.5 million by 2010. By 2012, it had further increased to 53 million, indicating a significant rise over the 42-year period.

Overall, the data shows a consistent and substantial increase in the Hispanic population in the U.S. from 1970 to 2012, highlighting the growing demographic significance of this group in the country.

The data reveals a significant upward trend in the percentage of births to unmarried women over the nearly four-decade span. The most notable increase occurred between 2000 and 2010, where the percentage jumped from 33.2% to 40.8%. This decade saw a particularly sharp rise, indicating a more rapid change in societal norms and behaviors.

Between 2010 and 2018, the percentage continued to climb, albeit at a slower rate, from 40.8% to 39.6%. This period shows a stabilization in the trend, suggesting that while the increase continues, it is not as rapid as in the previous decade.

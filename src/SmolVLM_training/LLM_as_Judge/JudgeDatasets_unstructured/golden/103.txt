Ireland : National debt from 2014 to 2024 ( in billion U.S. dollars )

The bar chart illustrates the national debt of Ireland from 2014 to 2024, measured in billion U.S. dollars.

The data reveals a gradual increase in Irelands national debt from 2014 to 2018, starting at 223.78 billion dollars and reaching 226.92 billion dollars. This upward trend continued into 2019 and 2020, with the debt rising to 229.83 billion and 227.68 billion dollars respectively.

From 2021 onwards, the projections indicate a more pronounced increase, peaking at 231.85 billion dollars in 2021. Although there is a slight decline expected in 2022 and 2023, with the debt falling to 230.52 billion and 228.22 billion dollars, the overall trend remains upward. By 2024, the national debt is forecasted to be 224.31 billion dollars, still higher than the 2014 figure.

Overall, Irelands national debt has shown a consistent upward trajectory from 2014 to 2024, with a notable peak in 2021. Despite minor fluctuations, the general trend suggests a steady increase in the countrys debt burden over the decade.

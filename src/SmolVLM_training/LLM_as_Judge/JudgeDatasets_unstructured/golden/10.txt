Decline in U.S. album sales over time
The graph illustrates the decline in U.S. album sales, including digital formats, from 2004 to 2008.

In 2004, total album sales in the U.S. were approximately 650 million. Over the next four years, there was a consistent downward trend, with sales dropping to around 500 million by 2008.

The decline was steady, with no significant fluctuations or periods of stabilization. Each year saw a reduction in sales, indicating a continuous decrease in consumer demand or a shift in music consumption habits.

Overall, the data clearly depict a significant decline in album sales over the five-year period, highlighting a substantial shift in the music industry during this time.

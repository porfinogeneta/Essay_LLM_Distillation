Total contribution of travel and tourism to GDP in Finland from 2012 to 2028  ( in billion euros )

The bar chart illustrates the total contribution of travel and tourism to GDP in Finland from 2012 to 2028, measured in billion euros.

The data reveals a fluctuating trend in the contribution of travel and tourism to Finlands GDP over the years. In 2012, the contribution was 19.8 billion euros, which slightly decreased to 19.4 billion euros in 2013. The trend continued with minor fluctuations, reaching 18.1 billion euros in 2015, the lowest point in the given period.

However, from 2016 onwards, there is a noticeable recovery and growth. The contribution increased to 18.3 billion euros in 2016 and further to 18.8 billion euros in 2017. By 2018, the figure rose to 19.2 billion euros. The most significant growth is projected for 2028, with an estimated contribution of 24.3 billion euros, marking the highest value in the dataset.

Overall, the contribution of travel and tourism to Finlands GDP has experienced a period of decline followed by a steady recovery and growth. The projected figure for 2028 indicates a robust rebound, highlighting the sectors potential to significantly bolster the national economy in the future.

The bar chart illustrates the expenditure of France and the UK on five consumer goods (cars, computers, books, perfume, and cameras) in 2010. The expenditure is measured in pounds sterling.

In terms of cars, the UK exhibited a higher expenditure, reaching approximately £450,000, compared to Frances £400,000. Similarly, the UK surpassed France in book expenditure, with the UK spending around £400,000 versus Frances £300,000. The expenditure on cameras in the UK, just over £350,000, was more than double that of France, which was only £150,000.

On the other hand, France outspent the UK on computers and perfume. Frances expenditure on computers was slightly above £350,000, marginally higher than the UKs exact £350,000. For perfume, Frances expenditure was £200,000, whereas the UKs was under £150,000.

Overall, the UK spent more on consumer goods than France in 2010. Both countries allocated the most funds to cars, while the least was spent on perfume in the UK and cameras in France. The most significant difference in expenditure between the two countries was observed in cameras, with the UK spending substantially more.

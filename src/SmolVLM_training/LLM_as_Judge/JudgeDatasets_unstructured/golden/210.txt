Estimates of the global prevalence of chronic respiratory diseases as of 2007 ( in millions )

The bar chart illustrates the global prevalence of various chronic respiratory diseases as of 2007, measured in millions of people. The diseases included are allergic rhinitis, asthma, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), sleep apnea syndrome, and other respiratory diseases.

The most prevalent chronic respiratory disease is allergic rhinitis, affecting approximately 400 million people globally. Asthma follows as the second most common condition, with an estimated 300 million sufferers. Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) affects around 210 million individuals, making it the third most prevalent respiratory ailment.

Other respiratory diseases, including sleep apnea syndrome, have a significantly lower prevalence. Sleep apnea syndrome affects about 100 million people, while other respiratory diseases collectively impact around 50 million individuals. These figures highlight the substantial burden of chronic respiratory diseases on global health.

Overall, allergic rhinitis and asthma are the most widespread chronic respiratory diseases, affecting hundreds of millions of people worldwide. COPD also represents a significant health challenge, while sleep apnea syndrome and other respiratory diseases have comparatively lower prevalence rates. These statistics underscore the importance of addressing chronic respiratory diseases in global health strategies.

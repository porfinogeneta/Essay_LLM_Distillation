In Spain, three-in-four 
The pie chart illustrates the public opinion in Spain regarding direct democracy, based on data from the Spring 2017 Global Attitudes Survey conducted by the Pew Research Center.

A significant majority of 75% of respondents in Spain view direct democracy favorably, considering it a good way of governing the country. Conversely, 22% hold a negative opinion, believing it to be a bad method of governance.

A small fraction, 4%, of the respondents either did not know or refused to answer the question, indicating a minimal level of uncertainty or indifference towards the concept of direct democracy.

Overall, the data indicates a strong support for direct democracy in Spain, with three-quarters of the population endorsing it as a good governing method, while a smaller portion remains skeptical or undecided.

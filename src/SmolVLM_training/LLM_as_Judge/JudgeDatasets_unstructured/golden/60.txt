Corruption perception index score of Brazil from 2012 to 2019

The graph illustrates the corruption perception index score of Brazil from 2012 to 2019.

In 2012, Brazils corruption perception index score was 43, which slightly decreased to 42 in 2013. The score then rose to 43 in 2014 but experienced a significant decline in the subsequent years, reaching a low of 35 in 2018 and remaining constant in 2019.

The period from 2014 to 2017 saw a marked deterioration in Brazils corruption perception index score. Starting at 43 in 2014, the score plummeted to 38 in 2015, indicating a worsening perception of corruption. Although there was a brief improvement to 40 in 2016, the score again dropped to 37 in 2017.

Overall, Brazils corruption perception index score exhibited a downward trend from 2012 to 2019, reflecting an increasing perception of corruption within the country. The score peaked at 43 in 2012 and 2014 but declined steadily, reaching a low of 35 in 2018 and 2019.

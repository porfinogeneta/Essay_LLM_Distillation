The bar chart illustrates the expenditure on various consumer goods by France and the UK in 2010, measured in pounds sterling. The goods include cars, computers, books, perfume, and cameras.

In terms of expenditure on cars, the UK surpassed France, with spending reaching approximately £450,000 compared to Frances £400,000. Similarly, the UK outspent France on books, with expenditures around £400,000 and £300,000 respectively. The disparity was even more pronounced in the case of cameras, where the UKs expenditure was over double that of France, amounting to just over £350,000 versus £150,000.

Conversely, France exhibited higher spending on computers and perfume. The French spent slightly more than £350,000 on computers, marginally exceeding the UKs expenditure of exactly £350,000. Perfume expenditure in France was about £200,000, significantly higher than the UKs expenditure, which was under £150,000.

Overall, the UK demonstrated higher spending on cars, books, and cameras, while France allocated more funds to computers and perfume. The most notable difference in expenditure between the two countries was observed in the category of cameras, where the UKs spending was substantially higher than that of France.

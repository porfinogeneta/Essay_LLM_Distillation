US Iraq responsibility
The pie chart illustrates public opinion on whether the U.S. has a responsibility to address violence in Iraq, based on a survey conducted in July 2014.

A majority of respondents, 55%, believe that the U.S. does not have a responsibility to intervene in Iraq. In contrast, 39% think that the U.S. does have a responsibility to address the violence there. A small fraction, 6%, are unsure or do not have an opinion on the matter.

Overall, the survey indicates a significant skepticism among the public regarding U.S. involvement in Iraq, with a majority opposing the idea of taking responsibility for the violence there.

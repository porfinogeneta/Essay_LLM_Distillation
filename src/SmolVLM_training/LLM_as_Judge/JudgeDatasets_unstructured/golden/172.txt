Gross domestic product ( GDP ) of Bonaire in the Caribbean Netherlands from 2012 to 2017 ( in million U.S. dollars )

The bar chart illustrates the Gross Domestic Product (GDP) of Bonaire in the Caribbean Netherlands from 2012 to 2017, measured in million U.S. dollars.

The GDP of Bonaire exhibited a consistent upward trend from 2012 to 2017. Starting at 372 million U.S. dollars in 2012, the GDP saw a steady increase each year, reaching 428 million U.S. dollars by 2017.

The most significant growth occurred between 2015 and 2016, where the GDP rose from 415 million to 434 million U.S. dollars. This period marked the highest annual increase within the timeframe, indicating a robust economic expansion.

Despite the overall positive trend, the growth rate varied slightly across different years. For instance, the increase from 2013 to 2014 was more modest compared to the substantial leap observed from 2015 to 2016.

Overall, Bonaires GDP demonstrated a clear trajectory of growth from 2012 to 2017, reflecting a strengthening economy. The most notable surge in GDP occurred between 2015 and 2016, underscoring a period of significant economic advancement.

 This statistic shows the number of coronavirus (COVID-19) deaths in Sweden in 2020, by gender. As of December 8, 2020, there were 3,344 deaths among women, 3,952 deaths among men and 7,296 deaths among the total population.
The coronavirus (COVID-19) pandemic is an ongoing outbreak of coronavirus disease (COVID-19) caused by the transmission of severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2). The virus was first identified in Wuhan, China, in December 2019. The virus spreads primarily through contact with an infected person, and can also be transmitted through respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs or sneezes. The virus can also be transmitted through contact with contaminated surfaces. The first case of COVID-19 in Sweden was confirmed on January 3, 2020. As of December 8, 2020, there were 7,296 deaths among the total population of Sweden.

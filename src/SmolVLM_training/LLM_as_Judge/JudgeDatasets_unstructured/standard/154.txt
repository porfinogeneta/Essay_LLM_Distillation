 The share of adults ages 18 and older who are married has fallen from 72% in 1960 to 50% in 2016. The share of adults who are married has fallen more among younger adults than older adults. In 2016, 53% of adults ages 25 to 34 were married, compared with 50% of those ages 65 and older. The share of adults ages 35 to 44 who are married has fallen from 57% in 2000 to 50% in 2016. The share of adults ages 45 to 54 who are married has fallen from 60% in 2000 to 55% in 2016. The share of adults ages 55 to 64 who are married has fallen from 62% in 2000 to 58% in 2016. The share of adults ages 65 and older who are married has fallen from 65% in 2000 to 58% in 2016.

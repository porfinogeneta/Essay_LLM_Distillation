 The chart shows the percentage of people in Britain, France, Germany, Italy, Spain, Greece, Poland, and the Czech Republic who consider various economic problems to be very big problems.

The most significant economic problem for respondents in the survey was the lack of jobs, with 78% of respondents considering it a very big problem. Public debt and rising prices were also considered very big problems by a large majority of respondents, with 71% and 67% respectively.

The least significant economic problem in the survey was the rich-poor gap, with only 60% of respondents considering it a very big problem.

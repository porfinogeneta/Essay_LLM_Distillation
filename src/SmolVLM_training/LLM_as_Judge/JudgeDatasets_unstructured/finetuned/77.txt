 The bar chart illustrates the percentage of Canadian adults who consumed marijuana or cannabis in the past three months, by age group, as of December 2019.

The data reveals that the highest percentage of cannabis consumption was among individuals aged 25 to 34, with 26.9% reporting consumption in the past three months. This is followed by those aged 35 to 44, with 20.1% consuming cannabis.

In contrast, the lowest percentage of cannabis consumption was observed among individuals aged 65 or older, with only 6.1% consuming cannabis in the past three months.

Overall, the data indicates that cannabis consumption is more prevalent among younger age groups, with a significant drop in usage among older age groups. This trend suggests a generational shift in attitudes towards cannabis use, with younger generations more likely to consume cannabis compared to older generations.

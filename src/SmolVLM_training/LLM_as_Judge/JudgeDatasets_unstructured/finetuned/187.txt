 The pie chart illustrates the percentage of police officers who say their work makes them feel angry and frustrated, as well as those who do not frequently experience these emotions.

According to the data, 47% of police officers report not frequently feeling angry or frustrated with their job, while 30% frequently experience these emotions but do not frequently feel angry. A small percentage of 1% of police officers frequently feel angry but do not frequently feel frustrated.

The remaining 21% of police officers frequently experience both anger and frustration with their work, indicating a significant proportion of officers who encounter high levels of stress and negative emotions in their profession.

Overall, the data reveals that a substantial portion of police officers, approximately 68%, either do not frequently experience anger or frustration with their work or do so in a moderate manner. However, a notable minority, around 30%, frequently experience both anger and frustration, highlighting the emotional challenges faced by many police officers.

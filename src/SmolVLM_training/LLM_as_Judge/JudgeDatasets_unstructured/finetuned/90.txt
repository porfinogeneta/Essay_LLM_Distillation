 The bar chart illustrates the market share of the U.S. automotive aftermarket in 2012, segmented by channel. The data reveals that OEM - new car dealerships held the largest market share at 27%, followed by general automotive repair shops at 16%. Body, paint and interior repair/maintenance shops accounted for 11% of the market, while automotive parts & accessories stores and tire dealerships each contributed 11%. The remaining 7% of the market was attributed to other channels.

Overall, the automotive aftermarket in the U.S. in 2012 was dominated by OEM - new car dealerships, with general automotive repair shops and body, paint and interior repair/maintenance shops also holding significant market shares. Automotive parts & accessories stores and tire dealerships contributed a smaller portion of the market, while other channels accounted for a relatively smaller share.

 The chart shows the percentage of Trump administration stories that cited each source type. The data is based on a content analysis of news stories from national newspaper websites, radio, cable and network broadcasts and websites, and digital outlets about President Trump or his administration from January 21 to April 30, 2017.

The data reveals that the public’s voice was rarely heard in stories about the Trump administration. The highest percentage of stories citing a source was from the Trump administration itself, at 74%, followed by news media at 35%. The lowest percentage was from the Trump family, at 1%.

Overall, the data indicates that the Trump administration was the most frequently cited source in the stories, with news media being the second most cited source. This suggests that the administration was the primary source of information for the stories, while the public’s voice was less frequently heard.

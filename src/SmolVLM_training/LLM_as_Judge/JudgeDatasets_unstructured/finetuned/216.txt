 The bar chart illustrates the direct-to-consumer (D2C) e-commerce sales in the United States from 2017 to 2021, measured in billion U.S. dollars.

The data reveals a steady and significant increase in D2C e-commerce sales over the five-year period. In 2017, the sales amounted to 6.85 billion U.S. dollars, which rose to 10.73 billion U.S. dollars in 2018.

The sales continued to rise in subsequent years, reaching 14.28 billion U.S. dollars in 2019 and 17.75 billion U.S. dollars in 2020. By 2021, the sales had further increased to 21.25 billion U.S. dollars, indicating a robust growth trend.

Overall, the data highlights a remarkable increase in D2C e-commerce sales in the United States from 2017 to 2021, with a notable acceleration in the latter half of the period. This trend suggests a growing consumer preference for online shopping and the increasing adoption of D2C models by retailers.

 The chart illustrates the gross domestic product (GDP) per capita in current prices in Zimbabwe from 1994 to 2024, measured in U.S. dollars.

The GDP per capita in Zimbabwe has experienced a fluctuating trend over the years. In 1994, the GDP per capita was at 714.03 U.S. dollars. It gradually increased from 1994 to 2000, reaching a peak of 969.82 U.S. dollars in 2000. Subsequently, the GDP per capita declined, reaching a low of 553.38 U.S. dollars in 2008.

From 2008 to 2010, the GDP per capita experienced a sharp decline, falling to 975.85 U.S. dollars in 2010. However, it rebounded in the subsequent years, reaching a peak of 1,516.28 U.S. dollars in 2017. Subsequently, the GDP per capita declined again, reaching a low of 859.95 U.S. dollars in 2020.

From 2020 to 2024, the GDP per capita has shown a gradual recovery, albeit at a slower pace compared to the previous years. It increased from 859.95 U.S. dollars in 2020 to 934.97 U.S. dollars in 2024.

Overall, the GDP per capita in Zimbabwe has fluctuated over the years, with notable peaks and troughs. The most significant increases occurred in the early 2000s, followed by a decline in the late 2000s and early 2010s. The recovery in recent years suggests a positive trend, although at a slower pace compared to the previous decades.

 The bar chart illustrates the national debt of South Sudan in relation to gross domestic product (GDP) from 2015 to 2025.

In 2015, the national debt of South Sudan amounted to approximately 59.37% of the GDP. This figure has fluctuated over the years, with a notable increase in 2015, reaching 114.25%. Subsequently, the debt decreased to 48.19% in 2018, indicating a recovery trend. However, it has since risen again, reaching 65.21% in 2017 and 71.74% in 2020.

From 2020 to 2025, the national debt of South Sudan has continued to rise, with the highest value in 2020 at 71.74%. This upward trend is expected to continue, with the debt projected to reach 35.33% in 2025.

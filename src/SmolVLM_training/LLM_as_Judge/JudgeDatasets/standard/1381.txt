The foreign-born share of the U.S. population has been on an upward trajectory since 1850. In 1850, the foreign-born share of the U.S. population was 9.7%. This figure rose to a peak of 14.8% in 1890 before experiencing a significant decline, reaching its lowest point of 4.7% in 1970. Since 1970, the foreign-born share has been on an upward trajectory. By 2000, it had climbed to 12%, and projections indicate it will continue to rise, reaching 19% by 2050.

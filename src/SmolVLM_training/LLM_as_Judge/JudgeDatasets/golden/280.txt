Majority of Americans say COVID-19 has forced journalists to change how they report the news
1. <Describe a graph:>
"The pie chart illustrates the perspectives of U.S. adults on whether COVID-19 has compelled journalists to alter their news reporting methods, based on a survey conducted from April 20-26, 2020."

2. <Find the key points and trends:>

Paragraph 1:
"A significant portion of respondents, 28%, believe that journalists have had to change their reporting methods 'a great deal' due to the coronavirus outbreak. Another 43% feel that the changes have been 'somewhat' necessary."

Paragraph 2:
"In contrast, 19% of respondents think that the impact has been 'not much,' while 9% believe that there has been 'no change at all' in how journalists report the news. A small fraction, 1%, did not provide an answer."

3. <Write a summary of presented data:>
"Overall, the majority of Americans acknowledge that the COVID-19 pandemic has necessitated changes in journalistic practices, with 71% recognizing at least some level of alteration. However, a notable minority, 28%, perceives little to no change in reporting methods."

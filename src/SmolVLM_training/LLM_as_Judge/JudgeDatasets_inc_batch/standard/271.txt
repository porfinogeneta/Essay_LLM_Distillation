 A majority of Americans (44%) say marijuana should be illegal, according to a new Pew Research Center survey. But the reasons they cite for this view vary widely.
About half of those who say marijuana should be illegal say it is bad for individuals (43%), while 30% say it is dangerous, addictive drug. Another 19% say it should be illegal because it needs to be policed.
About a quarter (25%) say marijuana should be illegal because it is a gateway drug. And 11% say it is bad for young people.
About a quarter (23%) say marijuana should be illegal because it is illegal, but they also say that marijuana should be legal for medicinal purposes.
The survey was conducted March 25-29, 2015, among 1,001 adults. The margin of error is 3.4 percentage points.

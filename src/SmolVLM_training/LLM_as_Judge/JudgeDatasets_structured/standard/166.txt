 The number of applications for the Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA) program has fallen sharply since its launch in June 2012, according to a Pew Research Center analysis of data from the U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services.
In the first quarter of 2012, 228,582 applications were accepted for review. By the fourth quarter of 2014, the number had fallen to 26,995.

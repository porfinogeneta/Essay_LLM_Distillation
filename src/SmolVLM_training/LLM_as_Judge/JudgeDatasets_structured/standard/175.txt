 The public’s view of scientists is generally positive, but there are some differences in the way Americans view scientists based on their personality traits. For example, 89% of Americans say that scientists are intelligent, while 75% say they are focused on solving real-world problems.
The public also views scientists as good communicators, with 54% saying this about them. However, only 43% say that scientists feel superior to others.
The public is less positive about scientists’ moral values. Just 32% say that scientists pay attention to the moral values of society, while 29% say they don’t.

 The number of unauthorized immigrants removed from the U.S. rose 17% from 2017 to 2018, but remain below recent highs. In fiscal year 2018, 337,287 unauthorized immigrants were removed from the U.S., up from 322,281 in fiscal year 2017.
The number of unauthorized immigrants removed from the U.S. peaked in fiscal year 2008, when 432,281 were removed. Since then, the number of unauthorized immigrants removed has fallen, with the exception of a slight increase in 2016.
